# Week 1
# Topic Decision,Initial Research & Concept Planning

### Research 

This week focused on clarifying the overall direction of the project and revisiting foundational research related to emotional expression, visual symbolism, and body-based interaction. I reread several papers on visual semiotics and color psychology, which discuss how colors, shapes, and visual rhythms function as carriers of cultural meaning. In parallel, I reviewed research on body-interaction systems, especially studies on how Kinect skeleton tracking has been used in artistic installations.

I also revisited previous academic work related to visual emotion and audience perception, extracting two key principles:
	1.	Emotions are often perceived through abstract visual features such as saturation, density, and contrast.
	2.	Bodily movement naturally conveys emotional tendencies—for example, fast gestures are associated with anger or agitation, while slow gestures suggest sloth or fatigue.

These ideas revealed why the theme of the “Seven Deadly Sins” is a strong fit for a TouchDesigner + Kinect project: the sins are highly symbolic and can be mapped simultaneously onto both visual language and motion language.

---

### Development

Based on the research, I clarified why the “Seven Deadly Sins” ultimately became my chosen theme:
	•	Each sin carries a distinct emotional signature that can be translated directly into visual language.
	•	Each sin implies a recognizable body-movement tendency, which provides rich mapping possibilities for Kinect interactions.
	•	TouchDesigner excels at producing abstract, symbolic, mixed-media aesthetics that align naturally with the dramatic nature of the seven sins.

I created the project’s preliminary structural outline, including:
	•	The logic relationships between color, motion, and visual algorithms
	•	Parameter structures for all seven modules
	•	A full technical roadmap for the system
	•	A 12-week production schedule (Research → Visual Design → Technical Development → Integration)

---

### Reflection

The most significant progress this week was fully understanding why this theme is not only doable but meaningful. The theoretical foundation, visual directions, and motion-interaction pathways are now clear. Next week I will move forward to developing the visual language for each sin.
