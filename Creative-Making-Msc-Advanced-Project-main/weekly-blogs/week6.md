# Week 6
# Envy

### Research 

This week I moved on to Envy. I researched how envy is often described as unstable, restless, and sensitive. It is not an emotion of direct action, but one of internal tension and comparison. I also looked at glitch aesthetics and visual fragmentation as possible ways to express nervousness and insecurity.

---

### Development

While building Envy, I focused on making the system deliberately unstable. Even very small movements would cause strong visual reactions. The silhouette began to break apart, flicker, and fracture. I used noise-based displacement and fast opacity changes so that the image never fully settled. Unlike Pride, which felt calm and controlled, Envy constantly felt like it was on the edge of collapse. When I tested it, I found myself becoming more cautious with my movement because the system reacted so strongly to everything I did.

### Reflection

This week made me very aware that the system does not only respond to the body — it also shapes how the body behaves. Envy actively made me uncomfortable and tense, even though nothing “scary” was happening visually. This strengthened my understanding that emotion in this project emerges from the feedback relationship between body and system.
