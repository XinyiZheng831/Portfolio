# Week 7
# Wrath

### Research

For Wrath, I looked into how aggression, speed, and violence are often translated into moving images. I was particularly interested in how velocity and acceleration can become emotional triggers. I wanted to see whether fast movement alone could produce a feeling of anger without adding narrative content.

---

### Development

Wrath quickly became the most physically demanding system to interact with. I mapped movement speed directly to visual distortion intensity. The faster I moved, the more violently the image stretched, shattered, and erupted across the screen. Sharp trails and high-contrast edges made every action feel aggressive. Slow movements, on the other hand, felt like suppressed anger — the system stayed tense but did not fully explode.

### Reflection

Wrath helped me understand how powerful simple data mapping can be. I did not need complex visual effects to make the system feel intense. Speed alone was enough to completely change the emotional atmosphere. It also made me realise how physically exhausting emotional systems can become when they demand constant movement.
