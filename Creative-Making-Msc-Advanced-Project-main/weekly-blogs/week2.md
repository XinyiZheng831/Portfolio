# Week 2
# Visual Language Definition & Moodboard Development

### Research 

This week I focused on studying the visual characteristics and emotional signatures of each sin. I reviewed symbolic references in art history (such as Renaissance allegorical painting) and examined how contemporary visual artists use color and texture to construct emotional atmospheres. Film-color theory also provided references—e.g., “red = danger/hostility,” “gold = pride/excess,” etc.

I also explored research on emotional visualization in abstract digital art, focusing on:
	•	The role of color in psychological arousal
	•	The relationship between visual rhythm and tension
	•	How noise, distortion, and feedback contribute to emotional tone

This allowed me to design each sin’s moodboard with a coherent methodology rather than relying on intuition alone.

---

### Development

I produced the full set of seven moodboards, each including:
	•	Primary color palette
	•	Secondary colors
	•	Material references (silk, gold foil, smoke, glass, liquid textures, etc.)
	•	Visual dynamics (stretching, explosion, erosion, melting, diffusion, etc.)
	•	Potential TOP/CHOP structures
	•	References to relevant artists or artworks

Examples:
	•	Envy: acid green, mirrored structures, edge corrosion, contrast-heavy compositions
	•	Sloth: muted blue-grey, drag trails, blur, deceleration and stagnation effects
	•	Wrath: red tearing, shock-frame transitions, jitter, glitch distortion

I compiled all moodboards and notes into a shared Notion document, creating a “Visual Vocabulary” set for each sin.
