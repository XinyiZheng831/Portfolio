# Week 5
# Pride

### Research 

This week I officially began designing the first sin: Pride. Before building it, I spent some time thinking about how Pride is usually understood. Compared with emotions like anger or fear, Pride is quieter and more internal, but it still carries strong power. It is often linked to control, confidence, self-expansion, and dominance rather than chaos. I also looked at some visual references related to symmetry, light, and central composition, because I wanted Pride to feel stable and self-contained rather than explosive.

---

### Development

In TouchDesigner, I started shaping the first emotional system around the idea of stability and clarity. I focused on keeping the body centred and visually dominant on the screen. The silhouette stayed relatively clean, with very limited deformation even when the performer moved quickly. I used stronger brightness and glow to create a feeling of self-amplification rather than destruction. Compared to my early system tests in Week 4, this was the first time I truly felt I was designing behaviour, not just testing effects. When I stood in front of the system, my movement naturally became slower and more upright, as if the system itself encouraged a kind of controlled performance.

### Reflection

Pride became the first moment when the project really felt like it had an emotional identity. I realised that emotion does not always need dramatic distortion to be felt. Restraint, clarity, and stability can also be powerful. This week gave me confidence that each sin could become its own behavioural world, not just a new visual filter.
