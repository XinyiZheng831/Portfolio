# Week 12
# System Integration, User Testing & Final Preparation

### Research 

This week my focus shifted from designing individual sins to thinking about the entire interactive experience. I reviewed basic user interaction principles and paid more attention to how first-time participants might understand and navigate the system without explanation.

---

### Development

All seven sins were fully integrated into one modular TouchDesigner project. I unified the input system so that the same body data could drive all emotional networks in parallel. During testing, I originally planned to use different gestures to switch between each sin. However, users often triggered scene changes by accident. After several tests, I decided to use one unified gesture: making a right-hand fist to switch to the next scene. This made the interaction much more stable and controllable.

I conducted a small user test with five participants from different backgrounds. I did not explain the meaning of each sin in advance. I simply asked them to explore the system freely. Their reactions helped me fine-tune feedback speed, deformation strength, and visual sensitivity. Many participants described each scene as emotionally distinct even without being told what they represented.

### Reflection

This week marked the transition from development to final presentation. Watching other people interact with the system made me see my work from a new perspective. It confirmed that the emotional differences were readable without explanation. The project no longer felt like a technical experiment, but like a complete interactive artwork ready to be shown.
