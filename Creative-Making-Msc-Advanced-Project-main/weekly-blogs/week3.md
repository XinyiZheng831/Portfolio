# Week 3
# Emotional Mapping, Body Behaviour & Early Interaction Thinking

### Research 

This week built directly on my themed research and visual mood exploration from Week 2. After defining the Seven Deadly Sins as my emotional framework, I began focusing on how abstract emotional qualities could be translated into bodily behaviour and interaction logic.

I revisited how different emotional or psychological states are commonly associated with physical posture, rhythm, and spatial behaviour. Rather than treating these as fixed rules, I used them as soft references — for example, expansion often suggesting pride, contraction implying withdrawal or anxiety, and excessive movement relating to urgency or loss of control.

At this stage, I was less interested in “recognising” emotion through movement, and more interested in how emotion could later be constructed through visual behaviour once the body entered the system. This shift laid the conceptual foundation for separating body input from emotional output in later weeks.

---

### Development


Based on my research, I began sketching the first abstract motion-to-emotion relationships on paper rather than inside TouchDesigner. These were not technical mappings yet, but conceptual translations between bodily tendencies and emotional qualities across the seven sins. For example, I considered how pride could relate to vertical expansion, how sloth might be linked to delayed or minimal movement, and how greed could be imagined as continuous accumulation rather than release.

I also began thinking about how a user might switch between different emotional states inside the installation. At this point, switching logic was still very open — I explored ideas such as spatial zones, gesture changes, and sequential transitions, without committing to a final interaction method yet.

This week mainly functioned as a bridge between visual concept design and future technical development. Instead of making finished effects, I was building an emotional and interaction logic framework that could later be implemented computationally.

---

### Reflection

Week 3 helped me move from visual and thematic inspiration toward a more embodied and interaction-based way of thinking. Rather than imagining the Seven Sins only as visual styles, I started to think of them as behavioural systems that would later respond to the body in real time.

Importantly, this week clarified that the project should not focus on detecting what emotion the user feels, but on how different emotional qualities could be performed by the system itself. This conceptual decision directly shaped my later technical and artistic choices in TouchDesigner and became one of the core ideas of the final project.
