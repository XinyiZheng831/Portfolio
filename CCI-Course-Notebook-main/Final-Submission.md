**PROJECT DISCRIPTION**

 - **What You Made**: This project is an interactive 3D visual artwork themed around the concept of “information cocoons”. Users are immersed in a dynamic and evolving digital space where elements represent fragmented information(social media, news,advertisments,etc.), visualized as floating cubes connected. A central textured sphere symbolizes the individual absorbing information and are overwhelmed by the constant intake of information. The project combines abstraction with interactivity, encouraging audience to engage critically with the visualization and reflect on their role within these digital ecosystems. Moreover, It aims to provoke deeper questions about agency, bias, and the impact of algorithm-driven content delivery systems on human understanding and communication.

 - **How You Made It**: 
First, I used the preload() function to load textures and a looping video, ensuring that visual elements like the cubes and background were dynamic and ready before rendering. The 3D environment was initialized with a WEBGL canvas, enabling 3D transformations and smooth rendering. I generated 200 cubes with random positions, sizes, and textures, distributing them in a circular pattern using trigonometric functions (cos() and sin()) and storing their properties in an array for easy manipulation. To add vivid motion, I used ChatGPT for help to apply oscillating animations to the central sphere using sin() and rotated the entire scene with rotateX() and rotateY() based on frameCount. Each cube rotates independently, adding dynamic individuality. To emphasize the connections between fragments of information, I calculated distances between cubes with dist() and drew connecting lines using line() for pairs within a proximity threshold. Finally, I applied unique textures to the cubes and sphere using texture() and rendered them with box() and sphere() respectively, isolating transformations with push() and pop(). Finally, these elements create a visually cohesive and dynamic simulation of an individual trapped in fragmented information flows.



 - **Why You Made It**: The motivation behind this project is to explore how digital environments reflect the fragmentation and isolation of information consumption in the modern age. Inspired by the idea of “filter bubbles” discussed by Eli Pariser and the rapid spread of algorithmic content sorting, I try to use this project to visualize the invisible boundaries shaping human thought.By immersing myself in this project, I’ve deepened my understanding of data visualization and algorithmic art while critically reflecting on how technology impacts cognition and society. It also ties into my broader academic interest in critical design when it comes to coding and media studies, aiming to use art as a means to spark dialogue about complex societal issues.
