**SKETCHING**

《Disappointed(left) and Amazed(right)》

I created two abstract face(named"disappointed and amazed) conveying entirely different emotions by combining a variety of paper and digital materials. The collage elements are diverse, including facial features, backgrounds, and decorative details. These materials were sourced from various origins, such as fashion magazine models and facial features inspired by ancient Greek art. To ensure the edges of the pieces fit seamlessly, I used tools like Procreate for color adjustments and detailed refinements.

**RESEARCHING**

Each of these projects rethinks what a portrait can be, offering unique ideas that mix technology and art. Here’s what I liked and what I’d approach differently.

Positive Elements:

1 Memories of Passersby I by Mario Klingemann is inspiring because it generates endless, unique portraits using AI. The way it creates cohesive and intriguing images on its own aligns with my goal of combining randomness with meaning.
2 Reface by Golan Levin and Zachary Lieberman creates fun and interactive mashups of visitors’ faces. I like how it engages people and produces surprising results, which fits with my interest in playful, unpredictable outcomes.
3 Facial Weaponization Suite by Zach Blas critiques technology like facial recognition. I appreciate its bold approach and the way it uses art to make a political statement, which encourages me to think beyond aesthetics.

Negative Elements:

1 Responsive Face by Ken Perlin, though technically cool, focuses on sliders and parameters, which feels less emotional or symbolic. My goal is to create something more visually and conceptually striking.
2 Stranger Visions by Heather Dewey-Hagborg, while conceptually fascinating, feels ethically tricky. Using DNA to reconstruct portraits raises privacy issues that I’d prefer to avoid in my work.

I have created a collage program that generates new faces by using human facial features as the primary variables. It selects different source images and randomly combines them, resulting in entirely unique facial compositions each time. In this project, I used clock elements to represent different emotions for the facial features, such as “terrified eyes,” “peaceful mouths,” and “disdainful noses.” This approach allows the randomly combined individual parts to form a whole face that feels both bizarrely conflicting yet harmonious.
