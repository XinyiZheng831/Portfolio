# Week 2

### Labs

This week, I started building the Unity game framework and set up a basic racing scene. I imported a car model and added physics components (Rigidbody and Colliders) to prepare for acceleration, steering, and collision detection. I also looked into how to implement SerialPort communication in Unity to prepare for connecting with Arduino later.

---

### Reading & Reflections

Reading:

Celia Pearce’s argument about games as art struck me less for the usual “games deserve to be art” narrative, and more for her emphasis on the aesthetic of process and interaction. It reminded me that some of the most compelling games aren’t visually stunning, but emotionally sticky—because they let you co-create meaning through play. In Play Mountain, the idea that a slide is not a “function” but an “invitation” resonated deeply. A game, then, isn’t a completed object—it’s a space to be entered, explored, and enacted. Art not as display, but as invitation to action.

Working:

I deepened my understanding of Unity’s physics engine, especially parts related to car control. I realized that vehicle handling requires careful tuning of physics parameters to make the experience feel real. Although I haven’t connected the Arduino yet, I tested some SerialPort examples to get a head start.
