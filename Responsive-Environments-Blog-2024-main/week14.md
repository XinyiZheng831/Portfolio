

### Labs

For this week, after I created the moodboard, we were dedicated to finalizing all visual assets. Qin Pan finished the full-body illustration of the main character, multiple expressions,and variations of the spirit companion.I created the detailed scene backgrounds design including specific gradient color and objects, and did some related coding works. Every asset was exported in layers for later animation use.


Mood Board (By Me)

The mood board explores a dreamy and emotional aesthetic through a palette of warm yellows, soft pinks, and mysterious purples. The purple tones evoke a sense of illusion and darkness, while the sunset-inspired hues reflect hope, passion, and gentle vitality. It captures a delicate balance between softness and obsession, fantasy and reality. Keywords I drew from include passion, hope, softness, illusion.

For the final color scheme of the main scene, I chose a cooler purple tone combined with touches of dreamy pink to create a soft yet mysterious atmosphere. I also coordinated the scene’s colors with the character palette designed by Qin Pan, ensuring a more cohesive and balanced visual composition.She also gave me advice and direction for creating the mainscene. This core visual direction will also influence later parts of the game, including level design, item aesthetics, and UI transitions.

Before finalizing the scene, I tested several color variations. Although I initially considered using bright yellow and pink tones, they didn’t fit the soft, dreamy mood I aimed for. I also explored the idea of a multiverse with different color schemes, but due to time and consistency concerns, it wasn’t developed further. I still hope to revisit this concept in the future.

### Reading & Reflections

While refining assets, I thought about visual continuity. In narrative games, consistency between environments, characters, and UI strengthens immersion. I paid close attention to color harmony and symbolic repetition—like mirrors, curved shapes, and light direction.

---

### Peer Support

Qin Pan and I divided our responsibilities: she Pan is mainly responsible for the visual direction, including the opening animation and overall visual tone. I focus more on the technical execution, such as scene transitions and integrating motion effects into the illustrations components. This division helps us move forward efficiently while keeping a unified style.

---

### Project Development

The project entered its production phase visually. These visual assets will directly support coding, interaction, and animation in the next stages. It’s satisfying to see the emotional tone materializing through concrete artwork.
