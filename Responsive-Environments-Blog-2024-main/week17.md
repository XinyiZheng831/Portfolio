### Reading & Reflections

This week, I thought deeply about how simple mechanics can carry symbolic meaning. The act of changing letters became a metaphor for rewriting self-perception. It was fascinating to explore how interactivity can enhance emotional impact.

I also reflected on the balance between freedom and constraint in game design—too much freedom and the message is lost; too little, and it becomes a puzzle rather than a story.

---

### Peer Support

Pan Qing and I had detailed discussions about the emotional tone of the words we chose. We supported each other in testing different letter combinations and evaluating which transformations felt meaningful versus arbitrary. We also explored color palettes and audio cues that would better reflect the emotional shift in the gameplay.

---

### Project Development

This mini-game laid the emotional foundation for our project. We wanted players to feel a sense of agency in shaping their inner dialogue. It also helped us finalize the art direction and narrative structure for the rest of Echo of the Mirror World.
