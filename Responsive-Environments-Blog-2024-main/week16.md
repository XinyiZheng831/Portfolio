# Week 16

### Labs

Technical production ramped up this week. While Qin Pan refined the illustrations and rendered keyframes for the mirror world, I implemented interactive features using Unity. These included clickable items, collision triggers, and early versions of scene-switching logic.

For this week, I focused on integrating all the different scenes in Unity to create a smooth and coherent flow for the storyline. By linking the scene transitions logically, I was able to ensure the narrative progresses naturally, enhancing the player’s experience of the game’s plot development.

Additionally, I worked on adding subtle interactive details to some characters as well as objects,for example I use DOTween plugins to make "hoverup" effects . These small touches add a sense of atmosphere and make the environment "alive"  through various visual components. While doing this, I encountered some challenges related to different layers—some scenes involved UI elements, others default layers, and some had unique layer setups. Managing scene switches and triggers across these layers was tricky, but after some troubleshooting, I successfully resolved the issues, making the transitions seamless and stable.

### Reading & Reflections

Reading:

This week showed me how game developers draw from cinematic arts—storytelling, cinematography, and color—to boost immersion and emotional engagement. The materials emphasized that color is not just about aesthetics but a powerful emotional tool, while audio is crucial for atmosphere. The blend of games and film made me think that future immersive experiences may blur the boundaries between these two media even more.

Working:

I reflected on how interactivity should be emotionally cohesive with visual aesthetics. Even something simple—like the glow of an object as you approach—can become meaningful if timed well. I want to avoid “gamey” feedback loops and instead aim for something slower, more poetic.
