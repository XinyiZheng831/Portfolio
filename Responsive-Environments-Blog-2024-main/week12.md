Describe

1 What did you make?

I designed and developed an interactive level-based game called The Reviving Sound, centered around Peking Opera. In the game, players create and perform their own opera characters, engaging in traditional forms of performance including singing (Chang), narration (Nian), acting (Zuo), and martial arts (Da). The game integrates voice interaction, motion capture, and AR face-tracking to simulate the immersive experience of Peking Opera on a digital platform.

2 Why did you make it

Peking Opera, once a cultural peak in China, is now facing a decline in popularity, especially among younger generations. Most of its current audience are elderly, and it struggles to attract young people due to limitations in time, space, and format. I created this game to revive interest in this intangible cultural heritage by transforming it into a format that resonates with younger audiences—a digital, interactive, and immersive game experience.

3 What tools/materials/methods did you use?

• Game Engine: Unity – for programming game mechanics, scene transitions, character interactions, and audio feedback.
 • Motion Capture: Kinect – for detecting users’ body movements and mapping them to on-screen actions.
 • AR Technology: Real-time face tracking – to apply traditional Peking Opera masks onto the player’s face during performance.
 • Audio Analysis: Vocal pitch and tone detection – to evaluate the accuracy of users’ singing and narration.
 • Design Elements: Traditional opera components including roles, costumes, masks, and classic storylines (e.g., Farewell My Concubine, The Red Lantern).
 • User Testing: Iterative design and feedback loops to improve usability, engagement, and authenticity.

### Project Development

This week, we treated in-depth research as the core of project development. Before any aesthetic or mechanical decisions are made, it was crucial to understand the emotional landscape we want to address. I gathered global statistics from WHO (2021) and CDC reports, highlighting how 13–20% of adolescents worldwide experience mental health issues like depression and anxiety. These figures framed the urgency and relevance of our project’s emotional theme.

I also explored theoretical lenses such as “games as emotional medium” and the use of the multiverse as metaphor. The multiverse, in this project, serves as an emotional mirror—each world representing a different facet of the inner self. These conceptual frameworks are not just abstract theory; they are starting to shape how we design interaction, sound, and visual transitions.
