# Week 11

### Labs

This week we determined the concept of the project, titled Echoes of the Mirror World. We discussed the emotional tone of the experience and decided to build a narrative-driven interactive artwork that explores duality and inner reflection. Several keywords were extracted during brainstorming, such as “mirror”, “echo”, “soft time”, and “emotional healing”. We also established that the overall tone should be poetic, slow-paced, and immersive, rather than task-driven or gamified.

---

### Reading & Reflections

I revisited some references like Gris, Journey, and installation works like Dreams by Media Molecule. These works show how strong atmospheres and silent storytelling can guide player emotions without heavy text. I realized that our project could benefit from relying on visual language and abstract transitions to evoke meaning. Instead of linear narrative, we want something that the player can “feel” through visuals and audio shifts.

### Peer Support

Qin Pan and I spent time discussing what emotional state we wanted the audience to experience. She brought in some visual inspirations from Ghibli-style backgrounds and soft brush illustrations, which helped shape the mood. I focused more on the emotional structure and game feel, and we tried to balance our perspectives to find a shared vision.

---

### Project Development

We mapped out the skeleton of the mirror world concept: there would be a real world and a mirrored version, both slightly disjointed and unstable. The character will navigate both worlds, not through literal portals, but through emotional triggers and transitions. At this point, the interaction style is still open, but we want to keep it subtle and symbolic.
