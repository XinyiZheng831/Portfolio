# Week 19 Final Project

**Project Title：Echoes of the Mirror World -** 
---

## Overview & Project Description

Echoes of the Mirror World is an interactive narrative game built with Unity. The story follows a protagonist who travels through a series of parallel universes, each symbolizing a fragmented part of their emotional self. Players collect “echoes”—representing memories, feelings, or visual symbols—to gradually restore the character’s sense of identity. The game uses metaphors of multiverse and mirror worlds to explore inner healing, trauma, and emotional growth. Through immersive storytelling and visual transitions, players are encouraged to reflect on their own emotional experiences.

---
## Technical Description:

The game is developed using Unity with C# scripting. It includes multiple interconnected scenes representing different emotional states, and players can move between these scenes through trigger-based portals. Each scene contains interactive elements, such as objects that reveal dialogue or memories when clicked. A custom dialogue system was implemented to deliver story fragments, and Unity’s Timeline was used to coordinate certain cutscene-like moments. The game also uses 2D character animations, post-processing effects (e.g. color grading, vignette), and sound design to enhance emotional atmosphere. Scene transitions are handled through Unity’s SceneManager, and interaction logic is built using Collider and Raycast systems.

## User Test

Playtests conducted with:
We conducted playtests with a small group of six participants, including friends and classmates aged between 16 and 25. The testing involved short demos and interactive scenes designed to capture key moments of the gameplay and narrative.

Data gathered:
During the sessions, we collected qualitative feedback focusing on the emotional tone of the game and the clarity of its story. We recorded players’ verbal reactions, took screenshots to document their engagement, and conducted brief interviews to gather deeper insights.

Findings:
Overall, players responded positively to the symbolism embedded in the word-ladder game mechanic, finding it meaningful and engaging. The subtle animations incorporated in the storyline scenes significantly enhanced the sense of immersion, making the experience more vivid and emotionally resonant.

For reporting, we selected the most representative feedback from the six participants to accurately reflect the general reception and highlight key points for further improvement.

## Ethical Considerations

Given the game’s focus on identity and memory, ethical considerations around player experience were carefully reviewed. The design avoids manipulating player emotions in harmful ways and respects player agency by providing meaningful choices without deception. Privacy concerns were minimal as no personal data is collected. Future work may explore inclusivity, ensuring the narrative and interaction design consider diverse player backgrounds and accessibility needs, promoting an ethical and welcoming gaming environment.

---

## Design Justification 

While Echoes of the Mirror World successfully delivers an immersive narrative experience, certain limitations remain. The interaction methods are relatively simple and predominantly traditional, reflecting the game’s gentle tone and narrative focus; advanced input methods like sensors were not incorporated, which limits interaction diversity. Additionally, the project currently explores only one dimension within a parallel universe theme; there is significant potential to expand into other thematic universes, such as hope or more abstract, surreal spaces. This expansion would add greater depth and complexity to the work. We plan to address these aspects in future development.
