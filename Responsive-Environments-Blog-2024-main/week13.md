MainScene Scripts (By Me)

This sketch illustrates the main narrative scene of the game—a warm, home-like space inspired by the girl’s real-life environment. Familiar objects like a bed, bookshelf, and desk are deliberately placed to evoke comfort and recognition, while subtle surreal elements suggest this is a parallel world. This space symbolizes a chance for emotional rebirth, where the protagonist explores her surroundings, unlocks fragmented memories, and gradually moves toward inner healing and hope. The large doorway on the right hints at her eventual departure from this dreamlike world, marking the end of a transformative journey.

### Reading & Reflections

Reading:

This paper’s classification of technologies by immersion and interaction levels gave me a clearer framework to understand VR’s complexity. Revisiting Milgram and Kishino’s Reality-Virtuality Continuum felt like tracing VR’s evolution over time. Ryan’s chapter presents VR not just as tech but also as dream—a cultural narrative—offering a fresh perspective. The critique video on the Metaverse was a strong reminder that behind tech visions lie complicated capitalist dynamics, highlighting the tension between idealistic promises and reality.

Working:

This phase reminded me how visual design carries emotional weight. A face isn’t just a model—it’s a medium of empathy. I reflected on how subtle visual elements (like eye shape or scene layouts) can communicate vulnerability or courage.

### Project Development

This week, we mainly furthered the theme and details of our game. We decided to create a healing-style game, centered around a young girl who is struggling with emotion issues. Just when she’s about to give up on herself, she enters a new world — something like a parallel dimension. There, she is inspired by a small spirit, who gradually helps her rediscover the meaning of life. The detailed storyline is still under development, but we’ve started to outline the core concept. At the same time, we also began sketching some early concept art for the main character. Qin Pan sketched the first drafts of our main character’s portrait and the companion spirit. I was responsible for early composition for scene concept art—especially how space and scale might reflect emotional states.
