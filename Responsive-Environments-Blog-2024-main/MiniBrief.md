Presentation Content：

Slide 1 

Hello，everyone, today we are going to present our project，which is called Echoes of the mirror world, it’s an interactive narrative game designed to explore emotional healing through the metaphor of multiverse travel.

Slide 2 

This game invites players to step into the shoes of a character who moves between parallel universes—each one representing a different part of the self, or an emotional state that has been fragmented and forgotten. The player’s journey is about collecting these “echoes”—memories, feelings, or symbols—to slowly piece together a fuller sense of identity.

Slide 3 – Research

On the research of context, I was inspired by how many young people—especially teenagers—struggle with depression, anxiety, or a sense of being lost according to recent reports from the WHO and CDC. We also conduct playtests when it comes to participatory research.


Slide 4 

Our project is inspired by Everything Everywhere All At Once—not just its wild visuals, but how it talks about love, regret, and the meaning of ordinary life.
We also looked to Across the Spider-Verse, especially the idea of meeting different versions of yourself in parallel worlds.
Visually, games like GRIS and Celeste inspired us with their emotional tone and symbolic art style.
⸻
Slide 5 

We built our game using Unity. The structure includes a main storyline scene and several side elements, like dialogues, transition screens, and mini-games.
Since it’s a healing-focused game, we want players to experience emotional shift and mood changes throughout the journey. We reflect this through color palettes, visual style, and overall atmosphere.

Slide 6 – Character
Our main characters are a girl and a small glowing elf. The girl carries an emotional burden—who feels lost, overwhelmed, and disconnected from herself.
The elf, is a soft, glowing creature born from her subconscious. It represents hope, inner strength. We went through many drafts before settling the two characters.

Slide 7 – Scene
The main scene takes place in a dreamy, purple-toned parallel world. It’s soft and surreal, filled with fragments of everyday life and childhood memories. It feels like a space between reality and imagination—calm, nostalgic, and slightly mysterious.

Slide 8 – Animation1

To help players connect with the character, we created a short hand-drawn storyline. It’s divided into four scenes each showing a key emotional moment in the girl’s journey. 
In the first scene, we show a girl feeling overwhelmed by daily life—endless tasks, pressure, and noise. She starts to lose direction, feeling like everything is out of her control.

Slide 9 – Animation2

Next, she faces judgment from people around her—harsh looks, unwanted comments. These things pile up, and she slowly starts to break down under the pressure.


Slide 10 – Animation3

Then comes her lowest point. She feels stuck, unable to escape her emotions. For a moment, she even thinks about giving up completely.


Slide 11 – Animation4

suddenly time just stopped. Everything froze, like the world held its breath.  She’s pulled into a strange, dreamy world—quiet, soft, and mysterious. It feels like a second chance.


Slide 12 – Dialogue

When she first meets the glowing elf. They exchange a short, mysterious conversation, which then leads her into a different universe.
Dialogue plays an important role in our game. It’s not just about storytelling — it helps move the plot forward, offers gentle guidance to the player, and often includes comforting, healing lines that match the emotional tone of the game.


Slide 13 – Main Scene

In the main scene, players have a high degree of freedom — you can move around, explore the space, and interact with different objects to unlock side stories. The entrances to two mini-games are also hidden within this environment. Along the way, the character will engage in conversations with the elf, receiving guidance and subtle emotional cues.

Slide 14 – Game1

Our first mini-game invites players to transform hurtful or negative words into kind and encouraging ones — simply by changing a letter or rearranging the word. Through this small act of rewriting, players experience a quiet sense of healing, as if rewriting not just words, but the emotions behind them.

Slide 15 – Game2 

In the second mini-game, players journey through a dreamlike world to collect fragments of forgotten objects, but also memories, emotions, and symbolic moments. Each one helps her rediscover a sense of purpose, bringing gentle healing as she reconnects with what once made life feel meaningful.

Slide 16 – Challenges

We faced several challenges, like addressing sensitive mental health themes without being too direct. We used metaphors, soft dialogue, and resource links to keep it gentle. Balancing emotional depth with gameplay was also key, so we kept the mechanics light and symbolic. To make the multiverse concept clear, we used distinct visuals and sounds, with a central hub tying everything together.

Slide 17 Future

For future development, we plan to add AI-driven emotional responses, expand to mobile and VR platforms, and include an anonymous sharing space. We also hope to design more emotional universes—like hope, fear, and nostalgia—to deepen the journey. Thank you for listening.
