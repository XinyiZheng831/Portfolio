### Reading & Reflections

This game pushed me to think about how spatial interaction can evoke psychological experiences. The act of exploring and collecting pieces of memory mirrors therapeutic introspection. It also made me consider how gameplay pacing affects emotional tone—slow, deliberate collection encourages reflection, unlike fast-paced games.

---

### Peer Support


Pan Qing and I collaborated closely on the symbolic design of each memory fragment. We shared personal ideas for what each memory could represent—loneliness, comfort, fear, or joy—and translated them into short, visual or textual cues. We tested and gave feedback on each other’s layout and movement designs, refining the emotional journey together.


---

### Project Development

With both mini-games developed, our project now has two complementary systems: one focused on language and identity, the other on memory and healing. These two mechanics reflect the central theme of Echo of the Mirror World—the internal journey of reclaiming self-worth. We plan to unify them through consistent audio-visual language and narrative transitions.
